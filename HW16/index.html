Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Obesity

The poverty index and obesity rate have a correlation coefficient of 0.46, suggesting that poverty levels and obesity rates are moderately positively correlated. The r-squared value of 0.21 indicates that roughly 21% of the variation in obesity can be explained by variations in poverty.

These findings come at no surprise, as lower income individuals typically have more limited access to nutritious foods, and often are less educated around healthy diet and exercise habits.
