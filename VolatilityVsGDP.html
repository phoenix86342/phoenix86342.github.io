GDP Volatility vs GDP per Capita



To address this component, we wanted to test whether countries with higher GDP per capitas also tended to experience lower volatility in their GDP per capita. In other words, do wealthier countries tend to have more stable economies? Variation in GDP per capita does appear to be able to strongly explain variation in GDP year-over-year volatility. The relationship is strongly negatively exponential. Most countries are clustered around a lower GDP per capita range that also experiences lower volatility. However, all countries with extremely high GDP/capita have extremely low GDP volatility, and all countries with extremely high volatility have extremely low GDP/capita.
