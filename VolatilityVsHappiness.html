GDP Volatility vs Gross National Happiness



To address this component, we wanted to test whether countries with lower economic volatility (measured through average year-over-year change in GDP per capita since 1960) tend to experience higher levels of happiness. The plot does not indicate any correlation between the two variables, suggesting that more stable economies do not necessarily lead to a happier populace.
